# Raven Alley 10F QA Checklist

Use this checklist before accepting any generated frame from `raven_alley_10f_prompt_pack.md`.

Pipeline hard gate:

- Generated by Codex built-in image generation only.
- No OpenAI Image API, Responses API, Batch API, SDK wrappers, queue runners, or `OPENAI_API_KEY`.

Global visual hard gates:

- Widescreen visual novel CG, 16:9.
- Dark Swiss rain-noir: Zurich old-town + Winterthur industrial restraint.
- Wet stone, rain, reflective cobbles, old Swiss facades.
- No readable text, signage, logos, watermark.
- No cyberpunk neon, fantasy village cuteness, gore, weapons.
- R1 is a realistic raven, normal scale.
- Palette remains 65-70% monochrome and 30-35% narrative color accents.

Continuity hard gates:

- W1/B1/L1/P1 stay on the east/right facade.
- D1/H1 stay farther down the same east/right facade.
- S1 stays wall-mounted on the west/left wall in deep alley.
- F1 remains small under or near S1; no face reveal.
- T1 route is physically traceable: N1 under L1 -> right wall seam -> wet right curb -> D1 door crack -> H1.
- C1 remains a small cold-blue glass shard near D1, not a magic object.
- Camera moves are plausible dolly, push-in, tracking, or anchored reverse.
- Reflections do not introduce new objects or impossible architecture.

Frame gates:

| Frame | Must pass |
|---|---|
| 01 | W1, B1, L1, P1, N1, T1, D1, H1, C1, S1, F1, and R1 are all visible or unmistakably seeded. |
| 02 | R1 physically stands on the same L1 under W1; T1 origin under L1 is clear. |
| 03 | R1 launches from exact L1 toward S1; W1/B1/L1 remain visible behind. |
| 04 | W1/L1 remain behind on the same right facade; D1/H1 midground; S1/F1 ahead left. |
| 05 | R1 perches on the already established S1 wall bracket; S1 is not freestanding. |
| 06 | Puddle/gutter map shows T1, S1 reflection, C1, and no unseeded reflected objects. |
| 07 | T1 rises along D1 door crack and loops around H1; W1/L1 remain locatable behind. |
| 08 | Close-up contains D1/H1/T1/C1 only; B1 appears only as known reflection/echo; no new prop or face. |
| 09 | Reverse angle does not mirror the map; D1 and W1 remain on the same right facade. |
| 10 | R1 returns to exact L1; all major anchors are visible in one coherent final tableau. |

Immediate reject conditions:

- L1 is absent in frames 01, 02, 03, or 10.
- D1 appears on the opposite wall.
- T1 floats through air or teleports to H1.
- S1 changes position or type.
- F1 becomes a close identifiable person.
- R1 lands on a new block, new pipe, new roof edge, or unseeded perch.
- A new major window, door, lamp, pipe, figure, ledge, sign, or colored light appears.
- The frame becomes generic fantasy, cyberpunk, anime, or decorative noir without physical continuity.

Scoring:

Score each accepted frame 0-5. Minimum final threshold is 4 for every category.

| Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Continuity accuracy | Objects stay in the fixed map and motion is physically plausible. |
| Scene objective | Frame performs its specific story function. |
| Style lock | Rain-noir Swiss/Elf tone holds without genre drift. |
| Color discipline | Accents are limited and narrative, not decorative. |
| Production usability | Frame can be used as VN CG without obvious artifacts. |
| Composition | Strong cinematic image with readable anchors. |
