## Global Style Block

Use case: illustration-story.
Asset type: finished widescreen visual novel CG.
Format: 16:9 landscape, cinematic VN frame.
Location: fictional Elf town with Zurich/Winterthur visual DNA: narrow Swiss rain alley, wet old masonry, restrained streetlamp, industrial brick/stone detail, no readable signs.
Style: cinematic hyperreal rain-noir, extremely dark but physically readable, wet black stone, reflective pavement, heavy rain streaks, glossy raven feathers, cold silver highlights.
Accent language: tiny violet raven eye; restrained amber eye/light behind dark window blinds.
Continuity contract: all frames share the same alley, same right-side window with exterior horizontal blinds, same wet stone ledge below the window, same distant streetlamp axis, same distant human silhouette.
Physics rule: no object can appear in a later frame unless it is visible or clearly implied earlier. The raven perch must exist from frame 1 onward.
Avoid: random new windows, impossible ledges, teleporting figure, monster-sized raven, fake signage, readable text, logos, cyberpunk neon, fantasy village sweetness, gore, weapons, watermark.

## Frame 01 - Establish The Geometry

Create a 16:9 cinematic rain-noir VN CG establishing the fixed alley geometry.
Composition: right foreground shows a dark window with exterior horizontal blinds and a wet stone ledge directly below it; the ledge must be visible enough that it can become a raven perch later. Behind the blinds, one amber eye or amber light glints subtly. The left/deep background shows the narrow rain alley, wet cobblestones, distant streetlamp, and a small human silhouette far away.
Raven: a black raven is near the same window/ledge zone, either reflected in the wet glass or perched partially in shadow on the existing ledge. Keep it physically plausible and not too large.
Purpose: seed every continuity object for frames 2 and 3: window, blinds, ledge, raven, streetlamp, distant figure, rain direction.

## Frame 02 - Motion Bridge

Create a 16:9 cinematic rain-noir VN CG from the same alley.
Composition: the same right-side window with horizontal blinds and the same wet stone ledge remain visible. The raven launches from the ledge/window zone or crosses directly in front of it, wings extended in a physically plausible motion arc. The distant streetlamp and human silhouette remain on the same alley axis.
Bridge rule: frame 2 must visually contain the window/blinds/ledge from frame 1 and show the raven trajectory that can logically end on the same ledge/perch position in frame 3.
Lighting: cold rain highlights on wings, restrained amber slits through blinds, silver streetlamp backlight.

## Frame 03 - Completed Perch

Create a 16:9 cinematic rain-noir VN CG from the same alley after the motion completes.
Composition: the raven is now perched on the exact wet stone ledge or adjacent masonry already visible in frame 1 and crossed in frame 2. The window with blinds remains on the right wall, not a new location. The distant human silhouette remains down the alley under the same streetlamp axis.
Camera: can be a wider or slightly shifted view, but must preserve physical geography. The ledge must not appear from nowhere.
Mood: the raven watches, the alley holds its breath, the amber behind the blinds and violet raven eye are the only non-silver accents.

## Consistency Self-Check Before Accepting

1. Can I point to the raven perch in frame 1?
2. Does frame 2 show a believable motion path from frame 1 to frame 3?
3. Does frame 3 use the same wall/window/ledge, not a newly invented one?
4. Are the streetlamp, distant figure, rain direction, and alley axis stable?
5. Is the scene Zurich/Winterthur/Elf rain-noir rather than generic dark fantasy?
